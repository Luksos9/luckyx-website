1.When the GRC: Policy and Compliance Management application is installed, what GRC related list displays on the Third-party Risk Issue record?
  • APolicies
  • BPolicy Exceptions
  • CConfiguration baseline
  • DCitations
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Policy Exceptions Source ServiceNow: TPRM Architecture and Data Model PDF (page 10) If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM issue GRC Policy Compliance related list Expert Explanation The Third-party Risk Issue record (sn_vdr_risk_asmt_issue) extends the base GRC Issue table (sn_grc_issue). When GRC: Policy and Compliance Management is installed, the platform adds policy exception capabilities to all GRC issue records. Because TPRM Issues inherit from the GRC Issue table, they automatically gain the Policy Exceptions related list. This lets risk assessors formally link an accepted issue to an approved policy exception, maintaining an auditable record that the known control failure has been reviewed and sanctioned.

Why the Others Are Wrong Policies: Policies are associated at the control and entity levels, not surfaced as a related list on individual issue records. Configuration baseline: A CMDB concept for tracking configuration item states; unrelated to GRC issue records. Citations: Citations are child records of Authority Documents, tied to controls and policies, not to TPRM issue records.

Memory Tip Issues extend GRC Issues, so they inherit GRC features. When you install Policy and Compliance, every GRC Issue (including TPRM Issues) gains the Policy Exceptions list because exceptions are how you formally accept a known control failure.

Real-World Example A TPRM assessor finds that a cloud vendor lacks encryption at rest, creating a risk issue. Rather than closing the contract, the organization accepts the issue and creates a Policy Exception for 90 days while the vendor remediates. The Policy Exceptions related list on the TPRM Issue record links these two records, creating a traceable audit trail for compliance reviewers.
2.To what do third parties have direct access?
  • AUpdate and respond to issues and tasks.
  • BView other third party's assessment responses.
  • CModify issue remediation workflow.
  • DView other third party's issues and tasks.
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Update and respond to issues and tasks. Source ServiceNow Community: TPRM Third-party Portal If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM third party portal access issues tasks Expert Explanation Third-party contacts access the portal using the snc_external role, which restricts their view to records explicitly scoped to their organization. Within that scope, they can view, update, and respond to issues and tasks assigned to them: submitting remediation evidence, adding comments, and updating task statuses. They cannot see assessments, issues, or tasks belonging to any other third party because the platform's access controls enforce complete isolation between vendor accounts.

Why the Others Are Wrong View other third party's assessment responses: Strictly prohibited. Each vendor sees only their own data. Seeing another vendor's responses would be a data breach. Modify issue remediation workflow: Third parties can respond to the workflow but cannot alter its configuration. Only internal users with admin roles can change workflow logic. View other third party's issues and tasks: Access controls prevent cross-vendor visibility. This isolation is a fundamental security requirement of the portal design.

Memory Tip Third parties can see their own issues and tasks. Nothing else. The portal is a secure, scoped window, not a shared inbox. UI Navigation Path: Third-party Portal > Issues / Tasks What to look for: The Issues and Tasks sections showing only the logged-in vendor's assigned records.

Real-World Example A vendor's IT security lead logs into the Third-party Portal and sees two open issues requiring remediation evidence and three tasks with upcoming deadlines. They upload a penetration test report to close the first issue and mark the data classification task as complete. They have no visibility into the 15 issues assigned to a different vendor in the same portal instance.

4.As a final step before closing the Third-party Risk Assessment, what task(s) would the Risk Assessor typically do?
  • AFlag questions for follow up.
  • BReview comments and evaluates the risk.
  • CCreate issues within the assessment.
  • DPerform a tiering assessment.
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Review comments and evaluates the risk. Source ServiceNow Community: TPRM External Risk Assessments If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM risk assessment closing final review state Expert Explanation The TPRM assessment lifecycle moves through: Draft, Scheduled, In Progress, Awaiting Approval, and Closed. Before the Assessor advances the record to Awaiting Approval or Closed, their final task is to review all submitted questionnaire responses, read third-party comments, and evaluate the overall risk level. Intermediate steps like flagging questions or creating issues happen during the In Progress phase when reviewing individual answers. The final evaluation is a holistic judgment that determines whether the third party meets the organization's risk acceptance threshold.

Why the Others Are Wrong Flag questions for follow up: This happens mid-assessment when specific responses need clarification, not as the closing action. Create issues within the assessment: Issues are created during evaluation when problems are found, before the final review is complete. Perform a tiering assessment: Tiering happens at the start of the engagement process (IRQ/Inherent Risk Questionnaire phase), before an external assessment is even sent.

Memory Tip Assess last, act first. All the actions (flagging, issue creation, tiering) happen before the final review. The Assessor's closing task is always the holistic evaluation, not another action item.

Real-World Example After a cloud storage vendor completes a 45-question security questionnaire, the TPRM Assessor reviews all 45 responses, reads the vendor's comments on two flagged questions, notes that three issues were already created, and then performs a final overall risk evaluation, rating the vendor as Medium risk and closing the assessment to move it to approval.
5.Which of the following describes the Third-party Tiering process?
  • AA process to evaluate a third party's risk based on their responses to a questionnaire.
  • BA process to evaluate a third party's risk based on their performance.
  • CA process to evaluate a third party's risk based on the criticality of the services they provide.
  • DA process to evaluate a third party's risk based on their financial stability.
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: A process to evaluate a third party's risk based on the criticality of the services they provide. Source ServiceNow: What is Third-party Risk Management? If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM third-party tiering criticality services definition Expert Explanation Third-party Tiering is the process of classifying vendors into risk tiers (e.g., Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 based on the criticality of the services they provide to the organization. Factors include: access to sensitive data, the potential business impact if the vendor fails or is compromised, regulatory exposure, and the substitutability of the vendor. A payroll processor with access to all employee PII would be Tier 1 (critical); an office supplies vendor would be Tier 3 (low). The tier then drives the depth and frequency of risk assessments sent to that vendor.

Why the Others Are Wrong Based on their responses to a questionnaire: Questionnaire responses inform the Risk Assessment score, not the Tiering classification. Tiering occurs before assessments are sent. Based on their performance: Ongoing performance monitoring (KPIs, SLA tracking) is a separate activity within TPRM. Tiering focuses on inherent criticality, not observed performance. Based on their financial stability: Financial health is one sub-factor in some tiering frameworks but is not the primary basis. Criticality of services provided is the central criterion.

Memory Tip Tier = how critical, not how good. Tiering measures the potential impact of the relationship, not the vendor's track record or questionnaire scores.

Real-World Example A healthcare organization tiers its 150 vendors during annual review. Its cloud EHR provider is Tier 1 because a service failure would directly impact patient care for 500,000 patients. Its coffee machine maintenance contractor is Tier 3 because a service failure has no regulatory or operational impact. Both vendors receive assessments, but the Tier 1 vendor gets a 90-question annual assessment while Tier 3 gets a 10-question biennial check.

7.When a vendor risk issue owner selects Accept during the Respond state, what happens to the control's compliance status?
  • AThe control status changes to Accepted and is excluded from future risk calculations until the exception expires.
  • BThe control status remains non-compliant until the control is re-assessed after the exception is closed.
  • CThe control status automatically changes to Compliant when the exception is approved by the Compliance Manager.
  • DThe control status is set to Waived and the associated assessment score is recalculated excluding that control.
Show full explanation
Correct AnswerControl status remains non-compliant until the control is re-assessed SourceServiceNow Community: TPRM Speed Learning Remediation vs acceptance ExplanationIn the Respond state, the issue owner has two paths: Remediate (fix the root cause and close the issue properly) or Accept (acknowledge the risk without fixing it). Selecting Accept creates an exception record that formally documents the known control failure and the business decision to accept the associated risk. However, the control status remains non-compliant throughout the exception period. The issue stays in the Respond state until the exception is closed. Only a subsequent reassessment that returns a compliant finding will update the control status. This is intentional acceptance is a risk management decision, not a remediation action, and the compliance record should reflect the actual state of the control. Why Others Are WrongThere is no Accepted control status. Compliance Manager approval in Review closes the issue, not the control status. There is no Waived status in the OOTB model.

Memory TipAccept = exception created + control stays red. Only a passing reassessment turns it green.

  • AThe Third-party Portal consolidates all communications and artifacts to a single system of truth.
  • BThird-party Portal allows the Third-party Risk Assessor team to reassign parts of an Inherent Risk Assessment.
  • CThe Third-party Portal matches a database of documents to document requests.
  • DThe Third-party Portal reduces risk for an organization by enabling secure communications with the third-party.
  • EThe Third-party Portal can be leveraged by the third party to work with other partners.
Show full explanation
Correct Answers: The Third-party Portal consolidates all communications and artifacts to a single system of truth. The Third-party Portal reduces risk for an organization by enabling secure communications with the third-party. Source ServiceNow Community: TPRM Third-party Portal overview If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM third-party portal benefits consolidation secure communication Expert Explanation The Third-party Portal solves two problems simultaneously. First, it provides a single system of truth: all questionnaire submissions, document uploads, issue responses, and task completions happen inside ServiceNow instead of scattered across email threads, SharePoint folders, and spreadsheets. Second, it is a security improvement: replacing email (which can expose attachments and lack audit trails) with an authenticated portal that supports MFA, access controls, and scoped data visibility reduces the attack surface of the due diligence process itself.

Why the Others Are Wrong Allows the Third-party Risk Assessor team to reassign parts of an Inherent Risk Assessment: The IRQ is an internal assessment. Reassigning its components is an internal workflow action, not a portal benefit for external vendors. Matches a database of documents to document requests: No automatic document-matching feature exists. Third parties manually upload the requested documents. Can be leveraged by the third party to work with other partners: The portal is scoped strictly to the relationship between the subscribing organization and each of its third parties. After portal deployment, all 80 vendors submit directly into ServiceNow with MFA enforced, creating a complete audit trail and eliminating the risk of sensitive documents sitting in unsecured inboxes.

  • CNew
  • DSubmitted to Third-party
Show full explanation
Correct Answers: Finalize with Third-party. Submitted to Third-party. Source ServiceNow Community: TPRM Issue Management and Remediation If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM issue workflow internal states submitted finalize skip Expert Explanation The default TPRM Issue workflow includes these states: New, Analyze, Submitted to Third-party, Finalize with Third-party, Review, Closed. The states Submitted to Third-party and Finalize with Third-party exist specifically for issues that require external vendor involvement: sending the issue to the vendor for remediation and then finalizing it after their response. The issue is created as internal-only because remediation requires internal IT changes, not vendor action. The issue workflow skips "Submitted to Third-party" and "Finalize with Third-party," going directly from Analyze to Review once the internal fix is applied.

15.What does accepting an Issue mark?
  • AAn implication that the known control failure is unimportant.
  • BAn intention to create an exception for a known control failure or risk.
  • CAttempted remediation failed.
  • DSpecial third parties get an exception.
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: An intention to create an exception for a known control failure or risk. Source ServiceNow Elite: GRC Issue Management If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich TPRM GRC accept issue exception control failure Expert Explanation In ServiceNow GRC and TPRM, accepting an issue is a specific risk disposition action meaning the organization formally acknowledges a known control failure or risk and chooses not to remediate it immediately. Instead, they create a policy exception that documents the acceptance decision, its business justification, the approver, and an expiry date. Critically, the control remains non-compliant after acceptance: accepting does not fix the problem, it formally records the decision to tolerate it under defined conditions. This differs from remediation (fix the issue) or transfer (shift the risk to another party).

Why the Others Are Wrong An implication that the known control failure is unimportant: Acceptance is a formal, documented risk decision. It implies the opposite: the risk is known, evaluated, and consciously accepted with management approval, not dismissed as unimportant. Attempted remediation failed: A failed remediation would be documented in the issue notes or trigger a re-evaluation. It is not what the accept action records. Special third parties get an exception: Acceptance is based on the risk decision about the control failure itself, not on special treatment for specific vendors. Any issue can be accepted regardless of which third party is involved.

Memory Tip Accept = acknowledge the risk, don't fix it. The control stays red. Accepting an issue is like signing a waiver: you know the risk exists and you agree to proceed with it documented.

Real-World Example A vendor is missing multi-factor authentication on a secondary system. The risk team determines the system contains only anonymized test data with no PII exposure. Rather than requiring a 6-month MFA implementation project, the TPRM assessor accepts the issue with a 12-month exception, documents the business justification, gets the CISO's sign-off, and links the acceptance to the Policy Exceptions related list. The control remains marked non-compliant but the risk is formally acknowledged.
