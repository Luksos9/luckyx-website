Question 60 - Multiple ChoiceQuestion A Program Manager wants to see the total 'Actual Cost' for the entire Program. How is this calculated?Answer OptionsAnswer (Correct): It is a rollup of the 'Actual Cost' of all Projects and Demands linked to the Program.Answer: It is manually entered on the Program form.Answer: It is derived from the 'Budget' field on the Program.Answer: It is calculated only from 'Program Tasks'.0) SourceServiceNow Documentation - Program Management The documentation confirms that programs act as containers where financials from child entities (Projects and Demands) are aggregated to provide a consolidated view of costs and benefits.1) Correct AnswerIt is a rollup of the 'Actual Cost' of all Projects and Demands linked to the Program.2) Detailed Expert ExplanationIn ServiceNow’s Strategic Portfolio Management (SPM), a Program is essentially a strategic bucket used to group related Projects and Demands to achieve a common goal. Because the Program itself doesn't typically "execute" work (that happens at the project level), its financial health is a reflection of its constituents.The Actual Cost field on the Program form is a read-only, calculated field. ServiceNow utilizes a "rollup" logic (often triggered by business rules like Recalculate Program Finances) that monitors changes in the underlying records. When a Project records an actual cost—whether through processed expense lines, time cards, or manual updates—that value propagates upward.Technically, the system sums the actual_cost field from all records in the pm_project and dmn_demand tables that have their primary_program field set to that specific Program. This ensures the Program Manager sees a real-time, holistic view of the total spend without having to manually aggregate data from dozens of individual initiatives.3) Comparative Analysis (Why others are wrong)Manual Entry: This is incorrect because ServiceNow prioritizes data integrity through automation. Manual entry would lead to "stale" data and constant human error. In SPM, financials are designed to flow from the bottom (execution) to the top (strategy).Derived from 'Budget': Budget and Actual Cost are two different financial dimensions. The Budget is the allocated amount (what you are allowed to spend), while the Actual Cost is the realized spend (what you have actually paid). One does not calculate the other; rather, they are compared to show variance.Calculated only from 'Program Tasks': Program Tasks are usually administrative actions (like "Organize Steering Committee Meeting") and often do not carry the bulk of the project's operational costs. Ignoring Projects and Demands would miss 99% of the Program's actual financial footprint.4) The "Click" Mnemonic"The Program is the Piggy Bank." The Piggy Bank doesn't make money itself; it only holds the coins (Projects and Demands) you put inside it. To know how much is in the bank, you just sum up the value of every coin inside.

  • ATRUE
  • BFALSE
Show full explanation
0) SourceServiceNow Docs - User RolesDocumentation on role inheritance confirms that a role can contain other roles, creating a hierarchy.1) Correct AnswerTrue2) Detailed Expert ExplanationThis concept is known as Role Inheritance. In ServiceNow, if Role A "contains" Role B, any user assigned Role A automatically receives all the permissions of Role B. For example, the it_project_manager role contains the it_demand_manager and it_pps_admin roles.
