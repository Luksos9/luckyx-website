5.In a quantitative risk analysis if the Single Loss Expectancy (SLE) is $1000000 and the Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO) is 20% what is the Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE)
  • A$200,000
  • B$2,000,000
  • C$10,000
  • D$1,000,000
Show full explanation
0. Correct Answer:$200,0001. Primary Source (Zurich Release)Direct Documentation Link: Official Doc: Risk Score CalculationSearch Tip: If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich ALE calculation formulaOfficial Proof: "The Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE) is the product of the Single Loss Expectancy (SLE) and the Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO)."2. Detailed Expert ExplanationIn ServiceNow Risk Management, quantitative scoring follows the standard FAIR/ISO methodology. The math is straightforward: $\text{ALE} = \text{SLE} \times \text{ARO}$.In this scenario:$\text{SLE} = \$1,000,000$ (The cost if the event happens once).$\text{ARO} = 0.20$ (20% chance of occurrence per year, or once every 5 years).Calculation: $\$1,000,000 \times 0.20 = \$200,000$.This value represents the expected annual financial loss, allowing the organization to decide if the cost of a $50,000 control is justified to mitigate a $200,000 annual risk.3. Comparative Analysis (Why others are wrong)$2,000,000: Incorrect. This would be the result if the ARO was 2.0 (occurring twice a year).**$10,000:** Incorrect. This is a decimal placement error ($1M \times 0.01$).$1,000,000: Incorrect. This is the SLE itself, not the annualized value.4. The "Click" Mnemonic"ALE equals SLE times ARO" — Just remember the acronyms alphabetical order ($A = S \times A$) doesn't work, but the math always does.

Detailed Expert ExplanationServiceNow follows a consistent "Order" logic across the platform (Business Rules, UI Policies, etc.).
