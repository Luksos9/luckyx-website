2.What would be the next step for this problem:"Problem investigation had been previously closed, because the risk was accepted, in favor of using the workaround, instead of applying the fix.After a couple of weeks, the issue starts to occur more frequently, so management wants to re-visit the root cause analysis."

  • BProblem Manager clicks Re-Analyze on the Problem record
  • CProblem Assignee clicks Re-Open on the Problem record

Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Problem Manager clicks Re-Analyze on the Problem record Source Problem management lifecycle - ServiceNow Docs If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich Re-Analyze problem reopen Risk Accepted Closed state Expert Explanation In ServiceNow Problem Management Best Practice lifecycle, the Re-Analyze UI action is the only mechanism to reopen a problem. It is available on problems in both Resolved (Risk Accepted) and Closed states. Clicking Re-Analyze transitions the problem back to Root Cause Analysis, enabling the team to reinvestigate. Memory Tip No "Re-Open" exists on problems. The word is "Re-Analyze." No time limit. Anyone manager-level or above can do it.

Real-World Example A recurring network instability was marked Risk Accepted three months ago because a permanent fix was cost-prohibitive. The same issue returns with greater severity. The problem manager opens the closed record and clicks Re-Analyze. The problem moves to Root Cause Analysis. The team creates a new problem task and escalates for a permanent infrastructure fix this time.
3.Where are defined the timeframe conditions for sending an SLA breach warning notification ?
  • ASLA definition record
  • BDefault SLA flow
  • CSLA Properties application
  • DSLA trigger conditions
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Default SLA flow Source ServiceNow Docs: SLA Flow If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich Default SLA flow breach warning notification timeframe percentage Expert Explanation The Default SLA Flow (Flow Designer) is where breach warning notification timeframes are defined. The flow contains "Wait until X% of SLA Duration" steps at various thresholds (commonly 50%, 75%, 100%), each followed by a notification action. When the SLA clock reaches each threshold, the flow sends the configured warning notification. The SLA definition record holds the total duration and start/stop conditions but delegates the notification timing logic entirely to the Default SLA Flow.

Why the Others Are Wrong SLA definition record: Defines duration, schedule, start/stop conditions. Does not contain notification timing percentages. SLA Properties application: Stores global configuration like "Use business calendar" or "Enable SLA." Not notification timeframes. SLA trigger conditions: Define when an SLA starts and stops (e.g., start when state changes to New, stop when Resolved). Not related to notification timing during an active SLA.

Memory Tip SLA definition = what and when to start. Default SLA Flow = what happens while it runs. The flow manages the SLA lifecycle notifications, not the definition record. UI Navigation Path: All > Process Automation > Flow Designer then search for "Default SLA flow" What to look for: "Wait for SLA Duration Percentage" action steps at various thresholds (50%, 75%, 100%) followed by notification steps.

Real-World Example The default SLA flow is configured with: wait until 50% of SLA duration (no action), wait until 75% (send email to assigned agent), wait until 100% (send email to agent + manager). A P2 incident with an 8-hour SLA gets a warning email to the agent after 6 hours. If still open at 8 hours, the manager is also notified. Both timeframes are configured directly in the Default SLA Flow, not in the SLA definition record.
4.What is a characteristic of Major Incidents in the ServiceNow platform?
  • AThey are resolved exclusively through manual processes.
  • BThey only affect a single user.
  • CThey are incidents of high impact and urgency affecting a large number of users.
  • DThey can only be created manually.
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: They are incidents of high impact and urgency affecting a large number of users or critical services Source Official Doc: Major Incident Management - ServiceNow Xanadu If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Major Incident Management definition high impact urgency Expert Explanation A Major Incident in ServiceNow is characterized by high Impact and high Urgency, typically affecting a large number of users, a critical business service, or multiple systems simultaneously. ServiceNow provides a dedicated Major Incident Management workspace that enables coordinated response through a communication bridge, stakeholder notifications, and dedicated task tracking. Major Incidents are differentiated from standard incidents by their Priority (typically P1 or P2) and the business-level attention they require.

Why the Others Are Wrong Resolved exclusively through manual processes: ServiceNow Major Incident Management includes automation features such as automated notifications, Slack/Teams bridge creation, and stakeholder updates. Manual-only resolution is not a characteristic. Only affect a single user: Single-user impact is a characteristic of a low-priority standard incident, the opposite of a Major Incident. Can only be created manually: Major Incidents can be promoted automatically from existing incidents based on priority thresholds or via business rules, not only through manual intervention.

Memory Tip Major = Many users + Maximum urgency. P1 affecting 1 person is not Major. P1 affecting 1,000 people is.

Real-World Example At 9:05 AM, an email server outage is reported. Within 2 minutes, 600 incident tickets are auto-created from monitoring alerts. The system automatically promotes the incident to Major Incident status based on the volume trigger rule. The Major Incident Manager is paged, a communications bridge is opened in Microsoft Teams, and stakeholder notifications are sent every 30 minutes until resolution.
5.What is the name of Business Rule that triggers automatic group assignment on Incident, Problem or Change requests?
  • APopulate Assignment Group based on CI/SO
  • BAuto-populate ITSM Assignment Groups
  • CITSM Assignment Lookup Rule
  • DAutomatic Assignment for ITSM
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Populate Assignment Group based on CI/SO Source ServiceNow Community: Auto-populate Assignment Group using CI If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich Populate Assignment Group based on CI SO business rule Expert Explanation The OOTB business rule "Populate Assignment Group based on CI/SO" runs on the task table and applies to Incident, Problem, and Change records. When a CI is selected on a record, the rule checks the CI's Support group (for incidents/problems) or Change group (for changes) and writes it to the Assignment group field. If the CI has no Support/Change group, it falls back to the same field on the associated Service Offering (SO).

Why the Others Are Wrong Auto-populate ITSM Assignment Groups: A fabricated name. The exact OOTB rule name matters for exam purposes. ITSM Assignment Lookup Rule: Fabricated. Does not exist as an OOTB ServiceNow business rule. Automatic Assignment for ITSM: Fabricated. Does not exist.

Memory Tip CI/SO = Configuration Item / Service Offering. The rule name literally tells you its logic: use the CI first, then fall back to the Service Offering. UI Navigation Path: All > System Definition > Business Rules then filter by name "Populate Assignment Group" What to look for: The rule on the task table with condition checking for CI field changes.

Real-World Example An agent logs an incident and selects CI "Web Server PROD-01." The business rule immediately fires, reads the Support group on that CI record ("Web Infrastructure Team"), and populates the Assignment group field automatically. The agent never has to know which team owns which server.

This enables "fit for purpose" Change models where the governance rules, required fields, and approval conditions are specific to each change type. Using State Transition Models, the admin configures each model's allowed transitions independently, giving each change type its own governance path without affecting the others.

  • CAn error is displayed indicating the Assignment group field must be populated before executing the Assign to me UI action
  • DThe Assignment group field remains empty
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: The Assignment group field is populated with the agent's user group Source ServiceNow Community: Assign to Me changes San Diego If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich Assign to Me single group empty assignment group auto-populate San Diego Expert Explanation The "Assign to Me" UI action behavior in San Diego and later releases: when the Assignment group field is empty and the agent belongs to exactly one group, clicking Assign to Me automatically populates the Assignment group with that group AND assigns the record to the agent. 8.What are the main stages in the Incident Management lifecycle within ServiceNow? (Choose 3) (choose 3)
  • ACreation and Classification
  • BInvestigation and Diagnosis
  • CResolution and Closure
  • DPrioritization and Scheduling
  • EFeedback and Improvement
Show full explanation
Correct Answers: Creation and Classification; Investigation and Diagnosis; Resolution and Closure Source Incident Management - Now Learning If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich incident management lifecycle stages creation classification investigation resolution closure Expert Explanation ServiceNow's official training defines three stages in the Incident Management lifecycle. Creation and Classification: the incident is created, categorized, prioritized, and routed. Investigation and Diagnosis: agents investigate the incident, apply workarounds if available, and work toward a permanent fix. Resolution and Closure: the fix is applied, the caller confirms resolution (or auto-closure timer fires), and the incident moves to Closed state. Prioritization is a task within Creation, not a separate stage. Feedback loops are post-process activities.

Why the Others Are Wrong Prioritization and Scheduling: Prioritization is part of Creation and Classification. Scheduling is not a defined incident lifecycle stage. Feedback and Improvement: A separate ITIL continuous improvement practice. Not a stage in the ServiceNow Incident Management lifecycle.

Memory Tip Create it, Investigate it, Resolve it. CIR: three stages, one per action type.

Real-World Example A user reports they cannot access the VPN. Stage 1 Creation and Classification: incident created, categorized as Network, priority P2. Stage 2 Investigation and Diagnosis: agent pings the VPN server, checks logs, identifies a certificate expiry. Stage 3 Resolution and Closure: certificate renewed, VPN restored, user confirms access, incident resolved and closed after 4 hours.

  • AExecution Plans
  • BContent Items
  • CCatalog Items
  • DRecord Producers
Show full explanation
Correct Answer: Record Producers Source Record producers - ServiceNow Docs If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich Record Producer catalog item type task records target table Expert Explanation A Record Producer is a type of catalog item that creates a record on any specified target table when submitted. Unlike standard catalog items (which create sc_request, sc_req_item, and sc_task records), a Record Producer can create an incident, a change request, a problem, or any other table record directly. This makes Record Producers ideal for giving end users a friendly Service Portal interface to submit records that belong to ITSM modules without exposing those modules' native forms. The catalog variables are mapped to fields on the target table record.

Why the Others Are Wrong Execution Plans: Define fulfillment tasks and approval steps for catalog items. They are a fulfillment configuration component, not a catalog item type. Content Items: Display static information or announcements in the catalog. They generate no records of any kind. Catalog Items: Create sc_request and sc_req_item records through the request chain. Memory Tip Record Producer = produces records on any table. The name is the definition.

Real-World Example The service desk wants employees to submit incident reports through the Service Portal without seeing the incident form directly. A Record Producer called "Report an IT Issue" is configured with target table set to incident. When an employee submits it, ServiceNow creates an incident record with fields populated from the catalog variables (Description, Location, Category). The employee sees a friendly form; the agent sees a proper incident record.

13.On the Change request, what types of Conflicts are detected automatically ? (Choose 3) (choose 3)
  • AConflict with Assignee Shift Schedule
  • BConflict with Blackout Schedule
  • CConflict with Company Holiday Schedule
  • DAnother change for the same CI, at the same time
  • EConflict with Maintenance Window
Show full explanation
Correct Answers: Conflict with Blackout Schedule; Another change for the same CI at the same time; Conflict with Maintenance Window Source Conflict checking when scheduling a change request - ServiceNow Community If the link fails, search Google for: ServiceNow Zurich change conflict detection types blackout maintenance window same CI Expert Explanation ServiceNow Change Management automatically detects three types of conflicts when a change is scheduled. Blackout window conflict: the scheduled time falls within a defined blackout period (e.g., year-end freeze). CI Already Scheduled conflict: another change affecting the same CI is already scheduled at the same time. Maintenance Window conflict: the change is scheduled outside the applicable maintenance window for that CI or environment. The conflict checker runs and finds three issues: (1) a blackout is in effect until Monday due to a quarterly business close, (2) another change is already scheduled for the same switch at 9 PM Friday, and (3) the applicable maintenance window for network devices is Saturday 2-6 AM. All three conflicts appear on the change record as warnings for the coordinator and CAB to review.
