ServiceNow certification cost in 2026

Every exam fee, retake price, free exam attempt option, and hidden cost. This page covers what you will actually spend to get certified, from CSA through the CIS specialist track.

The short answer

CSA and CAD cost $300 each. Every CIS specialist certification exam costs $450. Retakes are half price: $150 for CSA, CAD, and CPOA, $225 for all CIS exams.

But the exam fee is only part of the cost. Training, practice materials, and study time add up. This guide breaks down every line item so you can budget accurately before registering.

The free CSA exam attempt

ServiceNow offers a free first-attempt CSA exam through their Now Learning platform. Complete the CSA learning path, which includes modules, quizzes, and hands-on labs, and ServiceNow grants you exam eligibility that covers your first attempt.

This is a real free attempt. It works at Pearson VUE like any paid exam registration. There are a few things to know:

  • You must complete the entire CSA learning path, not just parts of it
  • The free attempt covers only the first try. If you fail, the retake costs $150
  • The exam eligibility has an expiration date, typically 6 months from issue
  • It is not transferable to other certifications

For CSA specifically, this means your total exam cost can be $0 if you pass on the first attempt. That is unusual in the enterprise certification world. AWS, Azure, and Salesforce do not offer equivalent free paths for their core admin certifications.

No free attempts for CIS or CAD. The free exam attempt program only applies to CSA. Every CIS specialist exam costs $450 and CAD costs $300 out of pocket for the first attempt. Your employer may reimburse this, but ServiceNow does not offer a free path.

Retake policy and costs

If you fail, you can retake the exam after a 3-day waiting period. Retake fees are exactly half the original price:

  • CSA, CAD, and CPOA retake: $150
  • All CIS exams: $225

There is no limit on retake attempts. You can retake the exam as many times as needed. Each retake costs the same amount. ServiceNow does not increase the fee for multiple failures.

The retake cost is why preparation matters. Failing a CIS exam once costs you $225 in retake fees plus the time to study and reschedule. Failing twice costs $450 in retakes alone, which is more than the original exam. A $10 to $50 practice test is the cheapest insurance against a $225 retake.

Costs beyond the exam fee

Training: $0 to $3,000+

ServiceNow offers instructor-led training through Now Learning. These courses typically cost $2,000 to $3,000 per course. They are high quality but not required. Many people pass using free resources and practice tests.

The free alternatives: Now Learning has self-paced courses with labs for most certifications. The official documentation at docs.servicenow.com is comprehensive and free. YouTube has community-created content, though quality varies.

Practice tests: $10 to $50

Third-party practice tests on Udemy, including the ones from Lucky X, typically cost $10 to $15 when purchased with a coupon or referral link. The full price is higher, but Udemy runs promotions constantly. At the $10 price point, a practice test with 200 to 400 questions costs less than a lunch.

PDI (Personal Developer Instance): Free

ServiceNow provides free Personal Developer Instances through developer.servicenow.com. You get a full ServiceNow instance to practice on. No credit card required.

PDIs hibernate after 10 days of inactivity. When you log back in, they wake up and your data is still there. This is not a limitation for exam prep. You can request a new instance at any time if you need a fresh environment.

Study time: 40 to 120 hours

CSA preparation typically takes 4 to 6 weeks at 8 to 10 hours per week. CIS specialist exams take 3 to 6 weeks depending on your experience with that module. CAD takes 6 to 8 weeks because the scripting depth requires hands-on practice.

If you are studying during work hours, this cost is covered by your employer. If you are studying on personal time, the opportunity cost is real. Factor it into your decision about which certification to pursue first.

Maintenance: Free (if timely)

ServiceNow certifications must be maintained with each new platform release. The maintenance assessment is a short online exam (usually 20 to 30 questions) that covers what changed in the new release. If you complete it during the maintenance window, it is free. If you miss the window, you have to take the full exam again at full price.

ServiceNow releases two major versions per year. That means two maintenance windows per year per certification. Set calendar reminders. Missing a maintenance window is the most expensive mistake in the ServiceNow certification ecosystem because you pay the full $450 again.

Certification Maintenance Program (CMP) fee: $200 per year

Starting in 2026, ServiceNow introduced the Certification Maintenance Program (CMP). The annual fee is $200 and covers all your mainline certifications with a single payment. You do not pay per certification. One $200 payment maintains everything.

The 2026 payment window opened January 16 and closes April 16, 2026. If you do not pay by the deadline, your certifications expire. This is separate from delta exams. You need to both pay the CMP fee and pass your delta exams to keep certifications active.

For someone holding three or four CIS certifications, $200 per year to maintain all of them is reasonable. But it is a cost that did not exist before 2026, and many professionals are not aware of it yet. Add it to your annual certification budget.

Getting your employer to pay

Most ServiceNow partners and enterprise customers have certification reimbursement programs. Some things to know when asking:

  • ServiceNow partners are often required to maintain a certain number of certified employees. If you work at a partner, your certification directly affects the company's partnership tier. This is your strongest argument for reimbursement.
  • Enterprise customers benefit from having certified staff who can configure and maintain their instance without external consultants. Frame it as a cost-saving measure.
  • Ask before you register. Most reimbursement programs require pre-approval. If you pay first and ask later, you may not get reimbursed.
  • Some employers pay for the exam only if you pass. This makes practice tests even more important. A $10 practice test that prevents a failed first attempt saves you $450 out of pocket while you wait for reimbursement.

Frequently asked questions

Do ServiceNow certifications expire?
Yes. You must complete a maintenance assessment for each new platform release. The assessment is free during the maintenance window. If you miss the window, you retake the full exam at full price.
Is there a student or military discount?
ServiceNow offers discounted access through academic programs and the RiseUp with ServiceNow initiative. Check nowlearning.servicenow.com for current eligibility. Standard exam pricing through Pearson VUE does not have a standing discount.
Can I combine multiple discounts on one exam?
No. One exam eligibility or discount per exam registration. They cannot be combined or partially applied.
What payment methods does Pearson VUE accept?
Credit cards, debit cards, and exam vouchers. Pearson VUE does not accept PayPal, wire transfers, or purchase orders for individual exam registrations.
How long is the free CSA exam eligibility valid?
The free CSA exam attempt from Now Learning is typically valid for 6 months from the date of issue. Check the details in your Now Learning account for the exact expiration date.

The bottom line

CSA can cost $0 if you use the free exam attempt and free resources. Each CIS certification after that costs $450. The biggest hidden cost is not the exam fee. It is the retake fee when you are underprepared.

The math is simple: a $10 practice test that moves your pass probability from 65% to 85% saves you an expected $45 in retake costs (20% reduction in failure probability times $225 retake fee). That is a 4.5x return before you account for the time saved on rescheduling and restudying.
