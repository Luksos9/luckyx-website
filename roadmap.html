6 tips that change exam outcomes

From community forums, Reddit r/servicenow, and people who passed (and failed) these exams.

Hit 85-90% on practice tests before you book

Community consensus is consistent: don't schedule until you score 85-90% on practice exams. Not once. Three consecutive attempts above that threshold. Below 80% means you're guessing on too many questions. One person at 87% once isn't ready. Three times at 87% is.

The eBook is the real study guide

The on-demand training teaches you how to use the platform. The exam tests vocabulary and theory. The course eBook (unlocked after completing training on Now Learning) is the single most important resource. Community veterans are explicit: "Refer only and only eBook. All mainline exams are based on eBook only."

Multi-select questions give zero partial credit

"Select all that apply" questions award zero points for partial answers. If the correct answer is A, C, D and you pick A, C, you score nothing. Community members cite this as the single biggest point killer across all ServiceNow exams. When in doubt on multi-select, include the answer rather than exclude it.

Vocabulary traps are the #1 reason experienced admins fail

One letter changes the correct answer. "Incident" vs "incidents." "Record" vs "records." A 6-year ServiceNow admin failed CSA because (his words): "The exam is PURELY vocabulary-based and has trick questions based off an 's' here or an abbreviation there." Know the platform? Great. Now learn ServiceNow's exact phrasing.

Take the exam at a test center, not online

Online proctoring requires an external webcam (not your laptop cam), working audio that a proctor will interrupt to verify mid-exam, and a lockdown browser that has a habit of crashing. Multiple community members report 2-3 interruptions during their exam. Pearson VUE test centers have none of these issues. The extra 30-minute drive is worth it.

Don't trust practice tests that don't link to official docs

Quizlet, ClassMarker, random forum dumps, and most free practice tests contain wrong answers. One bad practice set can teach you incorrect information that costs 5-10 real exam questions. Only use practice tests where every explanation links back to official ServiceNow documentation so you can verify the answer yourself.

The 6-week study schedule

Works for CSA or any CIS exam. Beginners: high end. Experienced admins: low end. 40-80 hours total.

WeekHoursWhat to doHow to verify you're ready
Week 18-12hComplete on-demand course on Now Learning. Don't skip labs. Download the eBook.You can explain every section heading in the eBook table of contents.
Week 28-12hRead eBook front to back. Highlight undefined terms. Build anything unclear in your free PDI.Take first practice test cold. Below 60%? Normal. Write down every guess.
Week 38-14hFocus on weak domains. Re-read those eBook chapters. Redo hands-on labs for those areas.Practice test #2. Should see 10-15% improvement. Below 70%? More time on weakest domain.
Week 48-14hFull cycle: take test, review every wrong answer against docs, re-test with different set.Scoring 75-80% consistently. Can explain why each wrong answer is wrong.
Week 56-14hPractice tests only. Different sets. Focus on multi-select and scenario reasoning.85%+ on three different exams. Book your real exam for next week.
Week 64-8hLight review. Re-read blueprint. Quick PDI check. Night before: eBook, not practice tests.You feel bored reviewing. That's the signal.

Experienced professionals: Compress to 2-3 weeks at 10-15 hours/week. Don't skip practice test milestones. The exam tests vocabulary, not your ability to do the job.

Do certifications expire?

Yes. Delta exams release with each platform version. 10 questions, open-book, non-proctored, 60 min, up to 5 attempts. 90-day window. Miss it and your cert expires (full retake at full price). Annual maintenance: $200 per holder covering all held certs.
