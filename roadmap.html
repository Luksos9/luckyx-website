The exam day playbook

10 tips from people who've taken these exams. Patterns that change outcomes.

Hit 85-90% on practice tests first

Don't book until you score 85-90% consistently. Three consecutive attempts above that threshold. Below 80% means too many guesses.

The eBook is the real study guide

The training course teaches platform usage. The exam tests vocabulary. The course eBook (unlocked after completing training on Now Learning) is the single most important resource.

Study the exam blueprint the night before

ServiceNow publishes exact domain weights. If Change Management is 18% of CIS-ITSM, that's ~11 questions. Allocate review time to match.

Multi-select = zero partial credit

"Select all that apply" gives zero for partial answers. If correct is A, C, D and you pick A, C, you score nothing. Biggest point killer.

Watch for vocabulary traps

One letter changes the answer. The exam tests ServiceNow's exact phrasing, not your understanding. If two answers seem right, official terminology wins.

Use 90-second pacing

60 questions / 90 minutes = 90 seconds each. Stuck at 2 minutes? Flag it and move on. Most who run out of time got trapped on 5-6 hard questions early.

"Best practice" beats "technically works"

Multiple correct answers exist. The exam expects ServiceNow's recommended approach. Official docs over real-world shortcuts.

Test center beats online proctoring

Online needs an external webcam, working audio, a lockdown browser. Community reports interruptions mid-exam. Pearson VUE centers avoid all of this.

Use your PDI until exam day

Free at developer.servicenow.com. Available 24/7. Five minutes building something in a PDI replaces 30 minutes re-reading notes.

Don't trust unvalidated practice tests

Quizlet, ClassMarker, random dumps frequently have wrong answers. One bad set teaches incorrect info costing 5-10 real questions. Only use tests that link to official docs.

Do certifications expire?

Yes. Delta exams release with each platform version. 10 questions, open-book, non-proctored, 60 min, up to 5 attempts. 90-day window to pass. Miss it and your cert expires (full retake at full price). Annual maintenance: $200 per holder. Retake policy: 3-day wait after first failure, 30 days after second, 6 months for subsequent.

For experienced professionals: Compress to 2-3 weeks at 10-15 hours/week. But don't skip the practice test milestones. The exam tests vocabulary, not your ability to do the job.
